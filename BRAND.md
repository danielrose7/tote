# Tote — Brand Voice & Copy Guide

## Personality

Calm, confident, direct. Like a well-organized friend who shops across every store and has strong opinions about browser tabs.

Tote is a tool that gets out of the way. That should come through in the copy: no hype, no dark patterns, no manufactured urgency. We respect the user's time and intelligence — in the product and in how we write about it.

## Voice Qualities

- **Specific over general** — name real stores (Zara, IKEA, Etsy, Depop), name real frustrations (scattered bookmarks, duplicate gifts, forty browser tabs). Specificity is credibility.
- **Short sentences. Em-dashes for emphasis.** Not for filler.
- **Problem-aware, not problem-obsessed** — acknowledge the friction briefly, then move on to what Tote does. Don't dwell.
- **Trust the product** — don't over-explain or over-promise. Let the feature description do the work.
- **Active voice, present tense** — "Tote keeps everything in one place" not "everything can be kept organized."

## What to Avoid

**Empty words:**
- "effortless," "seamless," "powerful," "amazing," "incredible," "innovative"
- "elevate," "empower," "streamline," "revolutionize"
- "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "game-changer"

**Dated headline patterns:**
- "Stop [doing X]" — overused negative framing
- "Never X again" — slightly desperate
- "Finally, a way to..." — implies users have been suffering; a bit dramatic
- "The future of shopping" — says nothing

**Generic section headers:**
- "The problem" — unlabeled prose works better, or make it specific to the use case
- "How Tote helps" — too corporate; either drop the header or name what specifically changes

## Headlines

Lead with a clear, confident statement of what Tote does for this use case. Prefer forward-looking framing over problem-first framing for H1s.

**Patterns that work:**
- Scope clarity: "Your wishlist across every store you shop"
- Elimination: "One board. No more tabs."
- Specific scenario: "Shop together — without the group chat"
- Result-first: "Your renovation, organized room by room"

**Patterns to retire:**
- "Stop [doing X]..." — use sparingly if at all
- "Never X again..." — avoid

## Lead Paragraphs

Name the real scenario in concrete terms, then pivot to Tote. Keep it to 2–3 sentences.

**Before:**
> Wishlists scattered across Amazon, store accounts, text messages, and spreadsheets? Tote puts every gift idea in one place — and makes sharing effortless.

**After:**
> Amazon lists, bookmarks, texts to yourself, screenshots — gift ideas scatter the moment you save them. Tote keeps everything in one place, organized by occasion, ready to share.

## CTA Copy

- "Get started — it's free →" ✓ (clear, direct)
- "Try Tote — it's free →" ✓ (at page bottom)
- Avoid: "Start your journey," "Discover the power of," "Unlock your potential"

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Marketing / use-case pages | Confident, specific, warm |
| Feature descriptions | Functional, direct — let the feature speak |
| FAQs | Conversational, brief, honest about limitations |
| Error states / UI copy | Plain, helpful, never apologetic or cute |
| Tip callouts | Peer-to-peer — "Building a capsule wardrobe? Set a selection limit..." |
