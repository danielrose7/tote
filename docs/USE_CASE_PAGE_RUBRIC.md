# Use-Case Page Rubric

## Purpose

Define a scoring system to evaluate and improve use-case pages so they:
- drive discoverability (SEO)
- convert visitors into users
- act as entry points into product behavior
- support shareable/demo collections
- stay on-brand: calm, confident, direct

## Overview

Each page is scored across 8 categories from 0-5.

- Total score: `/40`

Use this rubric with [BRAND.md](../BRAND.md), not by itself.

## Score Interpretation

- `32-40` -> Excellent (ready for distribution)
- `24-31` -> Good (needs sharpening)
- `16-23` -> Informational only (low conversion)
- `<16` -> Needs rewrite

## Categories

### 1. Instant Clarity (0-5)

Question: Can a user understand the use case in under 5 seconds?

#### 5
- Headline is specific and outcome-driven
- Immediately recognizable scenario
- No mental translation required
- Reads like a clear product use case, not a clever campaign line

#### 1
- Generic or abstract ("shopping", "organizing")

### 2. Concrete Example (0-5)

Question: Is there a real, usable example?

This is the highest leverage category.

#### 5
- Real collection with real items
- Visible immediately
- Feels usable as-is
- Could be copied and used without modification
- Makes the product feel already in motion

#### 1
- Purely descriptive page

### 3. Actionability (0-5)

Question: Can the user take meaningful action immediately?

#### 5
- "Use this list" / "Start from this"
- Clear next step tied to use case
- Minimal friction to begin
- The page suggests a practical setup, not just a signup

#### 1
- Generic CTA ("Sign up")

### 4. Use-Case Specificity (0-5)

Question: Does this feel tailored to a real scenario?

#### 5
- Uses domain-specific language
- Matches real-world behavior (gift list, gear planning, room-by-room furnishing)
- Feels purpose-built
- Anchored to one dominant scenario instead of trying to cover everything

#### 1
- Generic / horizontal

### 5. SEO Alignment (0-5)

Question: Does this match real search intent?

#### 5
- Title matches how people search
- Includes long-tail phrasing
- FAQ answers real queries
- Search language fits naturally and does not sound stuffed or robotic

#### 1
- Internal naming only

### 6. Differentiation (0-5)

Question: Does it clearly beat alternatives?

#### 5
- Clearly better than bookmarks
- Clearly better than notes
- Clearly better than wishlists
- Difference is obvious without deep reading
- Explains how Tote helps people compare, shortlist, refresh, and decide

#### 1
- Assumes user already understands

### 7. Shareability / Loop Potential (0-5)

Question: Would someone share or reuse this?

#### 5
- Includes reusable template
- Feels like a resource, not marketing
- Encourages copying or adaptation
- Someone could send the page because it is useful, not just because it sells

#### 1
- Purely informational

### 8. Emotional Pull (0-5)

Question: Does the user feel "this is for me"?

#### 5
- Relatable scenario
- Clear payoff
- Light tension without negative tone
- Briefly names the friction, then moves forward into action

#### 1
- Flat / neutral

## Key Principle

Use-case pages should not explain the product.

They should function as working examples of the product in action.

They should also read as one coherent point of view, not a stack of individually-correct sections.

## Brand Guardrails

Before giving a page a high score, confirm that it matches [BRAND.md](../BRAND.md):

- Calm, confident, direct
- Specific over general
- Problem-aware, not problem-obsessed
- Decision-oriented, not feature-inventory copy
- Short, clean sections that feel written by one voice

Score more harshly when a page:

- sounds pieced together section by section
- repeats the same idea in slightly different words
- uses search phrasing that fights the brand voice
- lists features instead of showing the workflow
- adds headers that feel like content scaffolding instead of user-facing guidance

## Usage Guidelines

### For Existing Pages

- Score quickly; do not overthink
- Fix the lowest-scoring category first
- If the page feels sloppy or stitched together, fix voice and structure before adding more copy

### For New Pages

Do not publish unless:
- Concrete Example >= 4
- Actionability >= 4

## Prioritization Rule

If only one thing improves, increase Concrete Example to 5.

This unlocks:
- clarity
- conversion
- shareability
- distribution

If the page still feels fragmented after that, tighten the narrative spine:
- workaround
- decision
- Tote carries the decision forward

## Final Framing

You are not building content pages.

You are building entry points into product behavior.
