Why templates matter

Pre-made cloneable collections are the content engine that feeds SEO, drives the clone → signup funnel, and gives the curator a job to do at scale. Aim for 30–50 before the public launch push. Titles should map directly to real search queries people type.

Home

Home renovation is a core use case. Specific titles ("under $1,500", "small apartment") outperform generic ones in search and conversion.

Home office setup under $1,500

Massive post-remote-work search volume. Cross-store comparison is exactly what people need here.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Home office setup under $1,500". 12–15 items. Total should stay under $1,500 if buying everything. Sections: "Desk setup", "Seating", "Audio and video", "Lighting and accessories". Prioritize ergonomics and longevity over aesthetics. Must include a monitor arm, quality headset, and a proper chair. Each item gets a one-line note on the decision rationale.

Small apartment living room — furniture that does two jobs

Dense urban audience, high consideration purchase, strong sharing behavior.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Small apartment living room — furniture that does two jobs". 10–14 items. Every item must serve a dual purpose — storage + seating, coffee table + desk, etc. Sections: "Seating", "Tables and surfaces", "Storage that doesn't look like storage", "Lighting". Mix price points from IKEA to mid-range. Avoid bulky or over-decorated pieces. Each item needs a note on its second job.

Style & Wardrobe

Wardrobe and personal style is an existing use case. Cross-store comparison is a genuine advantage over Amazon wishlists or Pinterest boards.

Capsule wardrobe for working from home

Year-round, high WFH search intent. Specific angle beats "capsule wardrobe" alone.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Capsule wardrobe for working from home". 12 items. Clothes that look put-together on video calls but feel comfortable. Elevated basics only — no hoodies, no full business attire. Sections: "Tops", "Bottoms", "Layers". Gender-neutral framing where possible. Each item: one line on why it works on camera and off. Mix $30–$150 per piece, prioritize widely-available stores.

Men's wardrobe basics

Underserved on most platforms. High search volume, specific enough to rank.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Men's wardrobe basics". 15 items. The things a guy needs to stop re-buying or borrowing. No trends. Sections: "Foundation pieces", "Outerwear", "Footwear", "The extras people forget". Prioritize quality over brand. Each item gets a note on what to look for — fit, material, why this category matters. Price range $25–$200. Stores: UNIQLO, Everlane, Quince, Banana Republic.

Travel

Trip-specific packing lists are highly searchable and shareable. Cross-store comparison is a genuine advantage — shoes from one place, tech from another.

Packing for a surf trip

7–10 day trip, daily surfing. Specific enough to rank, shareable in surf communities.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Packing for a surf trip". 12–15 items covering a 7–10 day trip with daily surfing. Sections: "In the water", "Out of the water", "Travel essentials". Must include reef-safe sunscreen, a quality rashguard, and a travel board bag. Skip tourist-trap gear. Each item gets a note on why it earns its place in the bag. Mix $15–$200.

Carry-on only — 10 days in Europe

No checked bag, no regrets. High search volume, strong sharing behavior among frequent travelers.

Now
Curator prompt

Build a collection called "Carry-on only — 10 days in Europe". 12–15 items that fit in a 40L carry-on. Sections: "The bag", "Clothing", "Tech and adapters", "Toiletries and travel essentials". Clothing: 3–4 mix-and-match neutrals. Tech: compact power bank, universal adapter, noise-canceling earbuds. Each item: one line on why it made the cut. No checked bag, no regrets.

Q4

Curation quality matters more than quantity

Titles should reflect real search intent, not marketing copy. Each collection should have 10–20 items minimum for SEO density. Descriptions should be specific ("for a small apartment") not generic ("for everyone"). All templates should be clone-enabled so visitors can immediately start their own version.
