# Use-Case Page Guide

## Goal

Use-case pages should improve organic discoverability by targeting search intent such as "gift wishlist app" or "home renovation organizer" rather than feature-description queries such as "how collections work."

These pages are not product explainer pages.

They should act as entry points into product behavior.

They should also feel editorially cohesive: one clear scenario, one voice, one through-line.

## Publishing Rules

- Score pages quickly; do not overthink
- Fix the lowest-scoring category first
- Do not publish a new page unless Concrete Example >= 4
- Do not publish a new page unless Actionability >= 4
- Do not publish if the page feels stitched together or off-brand, even if the numeric score looks acceptable

## Brand Checks

Use [BRAND.md](../BRAND.md) as a hard constraint.

Before shipping a page, check that it:

- sounds calm, confident, and direct
- names a real workaround, then moves quickly to decision-making
- uses specific stores, categories, and behaviors
- avoids feature-inventory copy when workflow copy will do
- does not lean on dated headline patterns or generic section scaffolding

If a page starts to feel piecemeal, cut or combine sections until it reads like one argument.

## Page Template

Each use-case page should follow this structure:

1. H1 with an outcome-driven headline
2. Lead paragraph with the pain point or desired outcome
3. Primary CTA
4. Short scenario framing: what workaround the user is using now
5. Practical setup or concrete example
6. Use-case-specific feature cards only if they move the story forward
7. "How it works" section with three concrete steps
8. Tip box with one power-user move
9. FAQ section with real search-intent questions
10. Related use cases
11. Final CTA

Not every page needs every supporting section. Fewer, stronger sections are better than a complete but stitched-together template.

## Content Standard

Prioritize working examples over explanation.

The strongest pages make the product feel already in motion:
- real collection examples
- real items or categories
- obvious next action
- reusable or shareable structure

The strongest pages also follow the brand workflow:
- current workaround
- decision to be made
- Tote helps carry the decision forward

Avoid copy that feels assembled from separate checklists:
- repeating the same promise in headline, lead, answer block, and cards
- broad "best for" or setup sections that do not add new information
- feature cards that restate workflow points instead of advancing them

When a page feels loose, simplify in this order:
1. tighten the H1 and lead
2. pick one anchor scenario
3. remove repeated sections
4. make each remaining section earn its place

## Use-Case Targets

### Gift Lists & Wishlists

- Route: `/use-cases/gift-shopping`
- Search intent: "gift wishlist app", "shared gift list", "birthday wishlist organizer"
- Core pain: wishlists scattered across store accounts, texts, bookmarks, and spreadsheets
- Story: building a holiday or birthday list, sharing it, and avoiding duplicates

### Home Renovation & Furnishing

- Route: `/use-cases/home-renovation`
- Search intent: "home renovation organizer", "furniture shopping tracker", "renovation budget tool"
- Core pain: too many tabs, unclear room-by-room decisions, budget drift
- Story: planning rooms, comparing options, and sharing with a partner or contractor

### Wardrobe & Style Board

- Route: `/use-cases/personal-style`
- Search intent: "wardrobe planner app", "fashion wishlist", "capsule wardrobe tracker"
- Core pain: saved clothing spread across stores and hard to compare later
- Story: building a seasonal wardrobe, curating by category, and tracking prices

### Shared Family Shopping

- Route: `/use-cases/family-shopping`
- Search intent: "shared shopping list app", "family wishlist", "collaborative shopping"
- Core pain: links are scattered across texts and purchases get duplicated
- Story: partners or families coordinating purchases for a shared project

### Professional Design & Client Projects

- Route: `/use-cases/professional-projects`
- Search intent: "interior design mood board tool", "contractor material organizer"
- Core pain: sourcing across multiple clients or projects is messy and hard to present
- Story: organizing options for approval and tracking budgets per project
